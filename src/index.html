CS422: Prototype Experiment

by Hai Tran (htran41@uic)

Dear participant,
Thank you very much for participating in my study. The purpose of this study is to compare different cursor types to see which of them help users select on-screen targets faster and more accurately.

What you need

  • A pointing device. A mouse is recommended.
  • A screen (laptop screen or desktop monitor) of at least Full HD resolution (1920 x 1080). A smaller screen size may greatly impact the result of this study.
  • Google Chrome. This study has not been tested on other web browsers.
Procedure
  • This study should take 15 minutes or less of your time.
  • Your task, in brief, is to click on the only green circle among many circles on the screen. When you have successfully clicked on the target, the circles will be re-located for the next trial.
  • After every 9 trials, you have 5 seconds to prepare for the next block of trials. A countdown will be displayed before the next group of circles appear on the screen.
  • After 27 blocks, your cursor type will be changed. There are two types of cursor: pointer and bubble. The pointer cursor works as a normal cursor, which requires you to hover on a target in order to click the target. The bubble cursor, however, allows you to click on the target as soon as the target is in range.
  • The study ends when you have finished all 54 blocks. Once you see a message which confirms the completion, you can safely close your web browser.
  • You can click the Start button below to begin the experiment.
WARNING: Please do not close the browser tab or resize the Chrome window during the study. Doing this may cause problem to the application; your progress will be lost after that and you will have to start the experiment from the beginning again.
